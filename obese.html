Workout Program for Obese Individuals

If you're obese and want to lose weight, gain strength, and improve overall health, your workout program should focus on:

  • Low-impact cardio – Burns calories without stressing joints
  • Strength training – Builds muscle and boosts metabolism
  • Consistency – Small, sustainable changes lead to big results

5-Day Workout Plan for Obese Individuals

Goal: Fat loss, endurance, strength, and mobility

Intensity: Start light and increase gradually

Equipment: Bodyweight, resistance bands, dumbbells

Weekly Plan:

  • Day 1: Low-Impact Cardio + Full-Body Strength
  • Day 2: Core & Mobility Work
  • Day 3: Upper Body Strength + Cardio
  • Day 4: Lower Body Strength
  • Day 5: HIIT (Modified) or Active Recovery
  • Day 6 & 7: Rest or Light Activity

Day 1: Low-Impact Cardio + Full-Body Strength

Cardio (20-30 min):

  • Brisk Walking, Cycling, Swimming, or Elliptical

Strength Training (3 sets each, 8-12 reps):

  1. Sit-to-Stand Squats (Use a chair)
  2. Wall Push-Ups (or Knee Push-ups)
  3. Seated Resistance Band Rows
  4. Glute Bridges (or Hip Thrusts)
  5. Standing Shoulder Press (Light Dumbbells or Bands)
  6. Standing Marches (Core Activation)

Day 2: Core & Mobility

Core Workout (3 sets, 10-12 reps):

  1. Seated Knee Raises
  2. Seated Russian Twists (Hold a light weight)
  3. Dead Bugs (Lying on back)
  4. Side Planks (Knee-supported) – Hold 20-30 sec

Mobility & Stretching (10 min):

  • Arm & Leg Circles
  • Cat-Cow Stretch
  • Seated Hamstring Stretch
  • Shoulder Rolls

Day 3: Upper Body Strength + Cardio

Cardio (20 min, low impact):

  • Walk, Swim, Bike

Strength Training (3 sets each, 8-12 reps):

  1. Seated Shoulder Press
  2. Seated Rows (Resistance Bands or Machines)
  3. Incline Push-Ups (Against a Wall or Bench)
  4. Bicep Curls (Dumbbells or Resistance Bands)
  5. Triceps Extensions (Dumbbells or Bands)

Day 4: Lower Body Strength

Warm-Up (5 min):

  • March in place, Leg Swings, Side Steps

Strength Training (3 sets each, 8-12 reps):

  1. Sit-to-Stand Squats
  2. Step-ups (Use a low platform)
  3. Glute Bridges
  4. Seated Calf Raises
  5. Side-Lying Leg Lifts

Day 5: HIIT (Modified) or Active Recovery

Modified HIIT (3 rounds, 30 sec each, rest 15 sec):

  1. Step Touches (Side to Side)
  2. Bodyweight Squats (or Sit-to-Stand)
  3. Wall Push-Ups
  4. Seated Leg Lifts
  5. Standing Shoulder Press

Alternative:

  • Light Activity – Walking, Yoga, Stretching

Days 6 & 7: Rest or Light Activity

  • Gentle stretching, deep breathing, or a slow walk

Tips for Weight Loss & Joint-Friendly Training:

  • Prioritize Low-Impact Exercises – Protects joints while burning calories
  • Strength Training is Crucial – Boosts metabolism & prevents muscle loss
  • Eat in a Healthy Caloric Deficit – Slightly fewer calories than you burn
  • Protein Intake – Helps with muscle retention (1.2-1.5g per kg body weight)
  • Stay Hydrated – 2.5-3L of water daily
  • Sleep Well – 7-9 hours per night
