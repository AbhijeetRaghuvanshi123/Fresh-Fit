Workout Program for Underweight Individuals

If you're underweight and looking to build muscle and gain healthy weight, your workout program should focus on progressive resistance training while ensuring proper nutrition and recovery. Here’s a beginner-friendly workout plan:

Goal

Gain muscle mass and strength

Duration

8–12 weeks

Frequency

3–5 days per week

Workout Split

Full-body (for beginners) or Upper/Lower split

Day 1: Full-Body Strength Training

  1. Squats – 4 sets x 8-12 reps
  2. Bench Press (or Push-ups) – 4 sets x 8-12 reps
  3. Deadlifts – 3 sets x 6-10 reps
  4. Overhead Shoulder Press – 3 sets x 8-12 reps
  5. Pull-ups (or Lat Pulldown) – 3 sets x 8-12 reps
  6. Plank Hold – 3 sets x 30-60 sec

Day 2: Rest or Active Recovery

  • Light cardio (walking, swimming, cycling)
  • Stretching or yoga

Day 3: Upper Body Focus

  1. Incline Dumbbell Press – 4 sets x 8-12 reps
  2. Barbell Rows (or Dumbbell Rows) – 3 sets x 8-12 reps
  3. Lateral Raises (for shoulders) – 3 sets x 12-15 reps
  4. Bicep Curls – 3 sets x 10-15 reps
  5. Triceps Dips (or Triceps Extensions) – 3 sets x 10-12 reps

Day 4: Lower Body Focus

  1. Deadlifts (or Romanian Deadlifts) – 4 sets x 8-12 reps
  2. Leg Press (or Bulgarian Split Squats) – 3 sets x 10-12 reps
  3. Hamstring Curls – 3 sets x 12 reps
  4. Calf Raises – 3 sets x 15-20 reps
  5. Core Workout (Hanging Leg Raises or Russian Twists) – 3 sets x 12-15 reps

Day 5: Full-Body or Cardio + Mobility

  • Option 1: Repeat Day 1 (Full-Body)
  • Option 2: 20–30 min cardio + stretching/mobility work

Additional Tips for Gaining Weight & Muscle:

  • Increase Calories – Aim for a caloric surplus (eat more than you burn).
  • Prioritize Protein – Eat 1.2–2.2g of protein per kg of body weight.
  • Eat Every 2–3 Hours – Include nutrient-dense foods like rice, eggs, chicken, beef, dairy, nuts, and healthy fats.
  • Drink Milk or Smoothies – High-calorie shakes help boost intake.
  • Track Progress – Increase weight gradually (progressive overload).
  • Rest & Sleep – At least 7-9 hours per night for muscle recovery.
